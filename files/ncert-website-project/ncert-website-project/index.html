What we do

Our operational departments

Five specialist teams work together to detect, understand, and neutralize threats to national digital infrastructure — from the network edge to the courtroom.

Cyber Security

ACTIVE

Our first line of defense — hardening systems and finding weaknesses before adversaries do.

  • Network security — firewalls, segmentation, and traffic inspection to keep intruders off internal networks.
  • Endpoint security — protecting laptops, servers, and mobile devices with anti-malware and device hardening.
  • Vulnerability assessment — systematic scanning to find and prioritize weaknesses before they're exploited.
  • Penetration testing — authorized simulated attacks that prove whether a defense actually holds.
  • Threat intelligence — tracking attacker tactics and emerging threats to stay ahead of the next campaign.
  • Security awareness — training staff and citizens to recognize phishing and social engineering.

Security Operations Center

MONITORING

The nerve center — watching every system, every log, every alert, every hour of every day.

  • 24/7 security monitoring — continuous watch over networks and critical systems.
  • SIEM — correlating security event data from across the organization into a single view.
  • Incident detection — spotting anomalies and confirmed intrusions in real time.
  • Log monitoring — reviewing system and application logs to catch what automated tools miss.
  • Threat hunting — proactively searching for attackers already inside the network.
  • Incident response — coordinating the immediate reaction the moment a threat is confirmed.

Software Development

BUILDING

Building the tools NCERT relies on — with security designed in from the first line of code.

  • Secure software development — following secure-by-design principles at every stage.
  • Web applications — internal dashboards and public-facing portals, built to withstand abuse.
  • Mobile applications — field and citizen-facing apps for reporting and alerts.
  • API development — secure interfaces that let systems and partner agencies share data safely.
  • DevSecOps — automated security checks built into every build and deployment pipeline.
  • Secure coding practices — code review standards that catch flaws before they ship.

Governance, Risk & Compliance

OVERSIGHT

The rulebook and the checklist — making sure security is consistent, documented, and provable.

  • Information security policies — the standards every department and system must follow.
  • Risk assessment — identifying, scoring, and prioritizing risks to critical systems.
  • Compliance management — keeping operations aligned with legal and regulatory obligations.
  • ISO 27001 — the international benchmark for information security management NCERT aligns to.
  • NIST Cybersecurity Framework — structuring how we identify, protect, detect, respond, and recover.
  • Internal security audits — regular independent checks that controls work as intended.

Digital Forensics Laboratory

INVESTIGATING

Where evidence is recovered, preserved, and made to stand up in court.

  • Computer forensics — recovering and analyzing evidence from compromised machines.
  • Mobile device forensics — extracting evidence from phones and tablets under strict chain of custody.
  • Memory forensics — examining live system memory for traces attackers try to hide.
  • Disk analysis — recovering deleted, hidden, or encrypted data from storage media.
  • Malware analysis — reverse-engineering malicious code to understand what it does and who's behind it.
  • Digital evidence collection — preserving evidence in a way that holds up to legal scrutiny.

How we operate

The incident response lifecycle

Every incident — large or small — moves through the same eight-stage lifecycle, so nothing is missed and every lesson makes the next response faster.

  1. 01

    Threat detection

    Automated tools and analysts flag suspicious activity across networks, endpoints, and applications.

  2. 02

    Alert generation

    Confirmed anomalies trigger an alert, routing the case to the right response team immediately.

  3. 03

    Incident analysis

    Analysts determine scope, root cause, and severity — what happened, how, and how far it spread.

  4. 04

    Containment

    Affected systems are isolated to stop the threat from spreading further while investigation continues.

  5. 05

    Eradication

    The root cause — malware, unauthorized access, or vulnerability — is fully removed from the environment.

  6. 06

    Recovery

    Systems are safely restored to normal operation, with additional monitoring to confirm the threat is gone.

  7. 07

    Reporting

    A full incident report is documented for leadership, regulators, and affected stakeholders.

  8. 08

    Lessons learned

    The team reviews what happened and feeds improvements back into defenses, tools, and playbooks.

About NCERT

A national mandate for cyber resilience

The National Cyber Emergency Response Team is the coordinating authority for cybersecurity incidents affecting national infrastructure, government systems, and critical services. We bring together technical specialists, policy experts, and forensic investigators under one roof so that when an incident happens, the response is fast, coordinated, and effective.

From day-to-day monitoring to large-scale incident response, NCERT exists so that citizens, businesses, and government agencies can trust the digital infrastructure they depend on.
